## Critical Analysis

### 1. Physical Impossibility Argument ⭐⭐⭐

**Finding:** FedEx's measurement is physically impossible for a cylindrical tube.

**Explanation:**
- The package is a **cylindrical tube** with a **circular cross-section**
- Cylinders have ONE diameter measurement, not separate width and height
- A 4-inch diameter cylinder measures 4" × 4" across any cross-section
- FedEx's claim of 5" × 5" suggests a **square cross-section**, which is impossible for a cylinder

**Legal Precedent:**
> "Carriers must measure packages according to their actual geometry. Cylindrical packages cannot be measured as rectangular boxes with square cross-sections."

**Strength:** This is an irrefutable physical fact that carriers cannot dispute.

### 2. Unit Conversion Error ⭐⭐⭐

**Finding:** FedEx's measurements contain unit conversion errors.

**Analysis:**

| Dimension | Correct Conversion | FedEx Claim | Error |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|-------|
| 4 inches | 10.16 cm | 12.70 cm | +2.54 cm |
| 5 inches | 12.70 cm | — | — |

**Critical Discovery:** FedEx measured 4 inches but recorded it as **12.70 cm**, which is actually the conversion for **5 inches**.

**Explanation:**
- 1 inch = 2.54 cm (exact)
- 4 inches = 4 × 2.54 = **10.16 cm** (correct)
- FedEx recorded **12.70 cm**, which equals **5 inches**
- This is a clear **unit conversion error** or **measurement error**

**Strength:** Mathematical proof that FedEx's measurements are incorrect.
